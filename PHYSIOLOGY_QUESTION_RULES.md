# Physiology Question Rules (System-Based, Extremely Hard)

These rules mirror the Anatomy/MedSurg standards, adapted for **physiology** (mechanisms + nursing-relevant interpretation).

## 1) Structure and Organization
- Build a **system-based** bank (Cardiovascular, Respiratory, Neurologic, Endocrine, GI/Hepatic, Renal/Urinary, Immune/Hematology, Musculoskeletal, Integumentary, Special Senses, Reproductive/OB, Fluids & Electrolytes/Acid-Base).
- Each system is subdivided by **Topic** (e.g., Hemodynamics, Autonomic Regulation, Gas Exchange, Acid–Base).

## 2) Difficulty Standards (Hard by Design)
Questions should be difficult **but fair**, emphasizing:
- **Cause → compensatory response** (baroreflex, RAAS, ventilation/perfusion changes)
- **Mechanism → bedside finding mapping** (hemodynamics to vitals, ABG patterns to ventilation problems)
- **Tradeoffs & time-course** (seconds vs hours vs days responses)
- **Competing mechanisms** (e.g., hypoxemia vs anemia vs shock; COPD vs CHF physiology)

Avoid definition-only items unless they are essential foundations.

## 3) Question Style Variety
Maintain a mix of:
- Standard single best answer
- SATA (select all that apply) with intentional, non-trivial distractors
- NGN Bowtie (cause → action → expected measurable response)

## 4) Answer Choice Rules (Differentiated Answers)
To prevent “all answers look the same”:
- Mix choice types (a mechanism, a variable relationship, an expected hemodynamic pattern, a compensatory reflex).
- Distractors must be plausible and physiologically adjacent.
- Avoid duplicated correct answers in a local set unless explicitly justified.

## 5) Nursing Relevance Requirement
Every question must connect to at least one nursing-relevant angle:
- What finding matters most and **why**
- What physiology explains the presentation
- What to monitor and what deterioration means

## 6) Rationales
For every question:
- Correct answer rationale (concise but mechanism-based)
- Brief “why wrong” for distractors when it adds value

## 7) Quality Controls (Non-negotiable)
- No ambiguous stems
- No repeated questions (concept + phrasing)
- Consistent “Extremely Hard” level
