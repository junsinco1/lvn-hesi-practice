General clinical reasoning

  • ABCs first: airway/breathing/circulation always outrank everything else.
  • High-risk cues: new confusion, SpO₂ < 90% with distress, SBP < 90, chest pain with diaphoresis, urine output < 0.5 mL/kg/hr.
  • Protocols: sepsis, stroke, chest pain—activate early, document time critical info, reassess frequently.
  • Documentation: objective data + direct quotes + actions taken (avoid labels/blame).

Nursing Math — core framework

  1. Write what you need (desired units): mL/hr, gtt/min, mg, units/hr, etc.
  2. Convert time: minutes ↔ hours (÷60 or ×60).
  3. Convert weight: lb → kg (÷2.2).
  4. Convert concentration: mg/mL, units/mL, mEq/mL.
  5. Cancel units (dimensional analysis) until only the desired unit remains.
  6. Round at the end unless policy says otherwise.

Gravity drips (gtt/min)

Formula: gtt/min = (mL × drop factor) ÷ minutes. Minutes = hours × 60.

IV pumps (mL/hr)

Formula: mL/hr = total mL ÷ hours. If ordered in minutes, convert minutes to hours first.

Vasoactive titrations (mcg/kg/min)

Steps: (1) mcg/min = ordered × kg. (2) mg/hr = (mcg/min ÷ 1000) × 60. (3) mg/mL = mg in bag ÷ mL in bag. (4) mL/hr = mg/hr ÷ mg/mL.

Reconstitution

Find final concentration after mixing (mg/mL). Then mL to give = ordered mg ÷ (mg/mL). If asked, remaining volume = total mL − drawn mL.

Peds dosing with max caps

Convert weight to kg, calculate dose, apply max dose cap, then convert to mL using concentration.
